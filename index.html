About

The number of resources available online for anyone who wants to learn web development on their own is vast, but it can be quite frustrating for a beginner the first time they set up a development environment, touch the command line, fix a merge conflict in git, miss a semicolon, or find that a section of a tutorial they were following is outdated.

I know because I've experienced all of these hiccups first-hand.

I didn't start learning until after I graduated from college, and what I've discovered since is that people -- mentors and co-workers and study partners at my same level -- were key to accelerating my learning.

Study groups help you:

  1. Stay motivated.
  2. Get un-stuck faster, because other people know exactly the issue you're running into with the tutorial.
  3. Take advantage of a safe environment where you can ask questions you might hesitate to ask in a public forum.
  4. Make connections and exchange helpful tips knowledge with peers who understand your background and skill level.
  5. Validate your understanding of the material when you find yourself explaining concepts to others running into the same questions you once ran into. (Receive mentorship, and mentor others in turn.)

We love Stackoverflow. We love Quora. We love r/programming and the online forums on Udacity and Coursera. Our hope is that with Code Buddies, you'll be able to get un-stuck faster -- and finally complete those tech goals you set for yourself as well. Join us to find a supportive group of peers.
