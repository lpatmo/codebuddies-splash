FAQ

What is CodeBuddies.org?

We're a community of people learning web development. Learning with others helps us learn faster. The project is free to join and open-sourced.

Do I need to pay anything?

No. It's a free and safe space. We help each other out.

What's the inspiration behind CodeBuddies.org?

The number of resources available online for anyone who wants to learn to code on their own is vast, but it can be quite frustrating for a beginner the first time they set up a development environment, touch the command line, fix a merge conflict in git, miss a semicolon, or find that a section of a tutorial they were following is outdated.

Oftentimes, people -- mentors, co-workers, hackathon buddies, and study partners -- are key to accelerating one's learning. But not everyone has access to study partners or mentors/co-workers who can help them.

Who can join?

Anyone can sign up as long as you have an internet connection! You don't need to be or aspire to be a career programmer. Code literacy is for everyone.

What should I do when I join one of the Google Hangouts?

  1. Turn off your video camera to save on bandwidth.
  2. Screen share by clicking on the green icon on the left (and the first window that appears after that). If everyone shares their screens, we can help each other more easily, see what we've each coded, and also be able to point out what we're talking about more easily.
  3. Take turns talking through the code out loud. Give everyone a chance to speak and ask questions. A good number for an efficient study session, in fact, is between 2-6 people -- all looking at the same shared screen or working through the same exercise(s).

What makes CodeBuddies different from Stackoverflow or a course?

  1. Our philosophy is peer-to-peer learning via Google Hangouts using audio and screen sharing. You learn from others, and also validate your understanding of web development or programming when you find yourself explaining concepts to others running into the same questions you once ran into.
  2. Everyone at the hangout is chatting about a specific topic or tutorial determined by the person who proposed the 1 or 2-hour-long study session.
  3. The experience may not be as rigorous as that of a coding bootcamp or paid course because meeting times are flexible; you can propose a series of hangouts to finish an entire free online book, but not everyone may stick with it until the end.
  4. The topics and times for study sessions can be proposed by any member of the community.

What's the history behind CodeBuddies?

Linda (@lpnotes) wanted to become a better web developer (and help others discover the joy of making things come alive on the internet, too).

CodeBuddies initially launched near the end of August 2014 as a set of Google Group mailing lists; the hangouts were proposed via individual google calendars. This setup was not ideal, since there was no way to see a list of all the upcoming hangouts, and one had to be invited to the Google Calendar in order to propose a new study session. The Google Group mailing lists did beget a lot of awesome knowledge sharing discussions, but there was no convenient way to search for them.

Just Launched

The platform (hangouts.codebuddies.org) launched on January 1st, 2015 after at least 50 hangouts, one in-person meetup in NYC in October, some late Christmas-New Year's Eve week nights, and feedback from some smart people.

How do I help?

The project is open-sourced, so check out the Github repo if you want an "open-source contributor" badge to your title and are interested in fixing one of the bugs. You can also file a bug yourself, or ask about a feature in the chatroom.

The best (and easiest) way to help, though, is to propose a study session.
